# Nexus Technologies Company Handbook

## Company Overview

Nexus Technologies is a mid-size SaaS company founded in 2018, headquartered in Austin, Texas. We specialize in workflow automation tools for enterprise clients. Our mission is to eliminate busywork so teams can focus on what matters.

**Core Values:**
- **Transparency** — We share context, not just conclusions
- **Ownership** — Every team member owns their domain end-to-end
- **Evidence-based decisions** — We measure, we don't guess
- **Customer empathy** — We ship what customers need, not what's flashy

## Departments

### Engineering
The Engineering team is organized into four squads: Platform, Integrations, AI/ML, and Infrastructure. Each squad operates semi-autonomously with a shared deployment pipeline. Engineering reports to the VP of Engineering, currently Sarah Chen.

### Product
The Product team manages the roadmap and conducts user research. Product managers are embedded within each engineering squad. The team uses a quarterly planning cycle with monthly check-ins.

### Sales and Customer Success
The Sales team is divided into Enterprise and SMB segments. Customer Success handles onboarding, retention, and expansion. Enterprise accounts have a dedicated Customer Success Manager.

### People Operations
People Ops handles recruiting, benefits administration, culture initiatives, and employee relations. The team is led by the Head of People, Marcus Rivera.

## Office Policies

### Office Location
Our primary office is at 1200 Congress Avenue, Suite 400, Austin, TX 78701. The office is open Monday through Friday, 8:00 AM to 7:00 PM CT.

### Remote Work
Employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week with manager approval. Remote work arrangements must be documented in the HR system (Workday). Fully remote positions are available for certain roles with VP-level approval.

### Hybrid Schedule
The default hybrid schedule is Tuesday-Thursday in office, Monday and Friday remote. Teams may adjust this with manager approval as long as at least 2 in-office days per week are maintained.

### Desk Booking
In-office employees must book a desk through the OfficeSpace app at least 24 hours in advance. Hot-desking is available on a first-come, first-served basis.

### Visitors
All visitors must sign in at the front desk and wear a visitor badge at all times. Employees are responsible for their visitors and must escort them throughout the visit.

## PTO and Time Off

### Vacation
Full-time employees accrue 15 days of PTO per year in their first two years, increasing to 20 days after two years and 25 days after five years. PTO accrues bi-weekly and carries over up to 5 days per year.

### Sick Leave
Employees receive 10 paid sick days per year. Sick leave does not carry over. A doctor's note is required for absences of 3 or more consecutive days.

### Holidays
Nexus observes 11 company holidays per year, including the standard US federal holidays plus a company-wide break between Christmas and New Year's.

### Bereavement Leave
Employees receive up to 5 days of paid bereavement leave for immediate family members and 3 days for extended family.

### Parental Leave
Primary caregivers receive 16 weeks of fully paid parental leave. Secondary caregivers receive 8 weeks. Leave must begin within 6 months of the child's birth or adoption date.

## Code of Conduct

### Expected Behavior
- Treat all colleagues, customers, and partners with respect
- Communicate professionally and constructively
- Respect differences in background, perspective, and identity
- Take responsibility for mistakes and learn from them

### Unacceptable Behavior
- Harassment, discrimination, or bullying of any kind
- Sharing confidential information without authorization
- Misrepresenting data or metrics
- Retaliating against anyone who reports a concern

### Reporting
Report concerns to your manager, People Ops, or via the anonymous ethics hotline at 1-800-555-0199. All reports are investigated confidentially.

## IT Security Policies

### Device Management
All company devices must use the approved MDM solution (Jamf for macOS, Intune for Windows). Personal devices may access company systems only through the approved VPN and browser-based tools.

### Password Policy
Passwords must be at least 16 characters and use a company-approved password manager (1Password). Multi-factor authentication is required for all company systems.

### Data Classification
- **Public** — Marketing materials, press releases
- **Internal** — Company announcements, meeting notes
- **Confidential** — Customer data, financials, roadmap details
- **Restricted** — PII, credentials, legal matters

Confidential and Restricted data must not be stored on personal devices or shared via personal email.

### Incident Response
Report security incidents immediately to security@nexustech.io. Do not attempt to investigate or remediate on your own. The Security team maintains a 24/7 on-call rotation.

## Expense Policies

### Reimbursable Expenses
- Business travel (flights, hotels, ground transportation)
- Client meals (up to $75/person)
- Team meals (up to $50/person, requires manager approval)
- Professional development (conferences, courses — up to $2,000/year)
- Home office equipment (up to $1,000 one-time for new remote employees)

### Expense Approval
Expenses under $200 require manager approval. Expenses $200–$1,000 require director approval. Expenses over $1,000 require VP approval.

### Submission Deadline
All expenses must be submitted within 30 days of the expenditure via Expensify. Late submissions may not be reimbursed.

## Performance Reviews

### Review Cycle
Nexus conducts formal performance reviews semi-annually in June and December. Managers are expected to provide ongoing feedback between review cycles.

### Review Process
1. Self-assessment (completed by employee)
2. Peer feedback (3-5 peers selected by manager)
3. Manager assessment
4. Calibration session (director-level)
5. Review conversation (manager and employee)
6. Goal setting for next cycle

### Compensation Adjustments
Compensation adjustments are made annually based on performance review results, market data, and company financial performance. Promotions are considered during both review cycles.

## Onboarding

### First Week
New hires complete a structured onboarding program including:
- Company culture and values orientation
- IT setup and security training
- Department-specific training
- Buddy assignment (a peer from the same department)

### First 90 Days
New hires have a 90-day onboarding plan with clear milestones. Managers hold weekly 1:1s during the first 90 days, transitioning to bi-weekly thereafter.
